TutorUnlock

A parental controls app that helps kids earn fun-time by spending time in learning apps.

How it works

A parent picks up to four learning apps or websites and chooses which fun apps to block. Fun apps stay locked until your child reaches the daily learning goal. After that, time spent in learning apps banks fun time at a ratio you set — for example, a 20-minute goal with a 30-minute reward earns 1.5 minutes of fun for every minute of learning.

Fun time works like a balance: it counts down while a fun app is open and goes back up as your child keeps learning. When the balance reaches zero, the fun apps lock again until more learning is done. You can also set an optional daily cap on total fun time, or grant a one-time bonus.

Common questions

How do I add a website (like Duolingo) as a learning app? In Parent Settings → Learning Apps, tap Pick learning apps & websites. In Apple's picker, add the website's address (for example duolingo.com) and tap Done. Back on the list, give it a short nickname. If you'd like tapping its ring to open the site directly, paste a full address into the optional Open link field — for example https://www.duolingo.com/learn. That link is what launches when your child taps the website's ring on the dashboard, taking them straight to the right page.
I'm locked out and need to get back into my apps. On the iPhone or iPad, long-press the TutorUnlock icon, tap Remove App, then Delete App. All shields release immediately.
My kid's progress isn't showing on the dashboard. Screen Time data can take a minute or two to register. Make sure Screen Time is enabled in iOS Settings and Family Controls access was granted on first launch.
I picked an app but it's not showing up as a ring. Only the first four apps or websites you pick get individual progress rings. Pick fewer, or remove some from the learning-apps picker to make room.
The kid recognizes the icons but not the names. In Parent Settings → Learning apps, type a short kid-friendly nickname next to each picked app or website. That nickname appears under each ring on the dashboard.

Privacy

TutorUnlock stores all data locally on the device. There are no servers, no analytics, no telemetry. Screen Time usage data is provided by iOS through Apple's Family Controls API and stays entirely under your control. Nothing leaves the device.
